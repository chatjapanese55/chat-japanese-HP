Course Overview

Bridge the gap between classroom and real life

This is a comprehensive immersion program for those who want to understand the heart of Japan — not just pass a test. Two tracks run in parallel throughout the week: Linguistic Connection builds your speaking confidence through daily conversation-first lessons, while Cultural Connection takes you into the city on weekends to use what you've learned where it matters most.

Designed for absolute beginners through intermediate learners — travelers, explorers, and anyone seeking genuine Japanese connections.

Who Is This For?

Built for travelers who want more

Proficiency Level

Absolute Beginner to Intermediate

No prior Japanese knowledge required. We assess your level on Day 1 and adapt the curriculum to your group — so you're never bored or overwhelmed.

Ideal Candidate

Travelers seeking real connection

You're not here to collect vocabulary lists. You want to walk into a bar, a market, or a festival and actually connect with the people around you.

Academic Features

Adaptive Learning — no two courses are the same

Your journey begins with a deep-dive orientation on Day 1. From there, every lesson is shaped around your group's goals.

1

Initial Assessment

We assess not just your grammar level, but your personal interests and social goals — what kind of conversations do you want to have in Japan?

2

Day 1: Self-Introduction

Every course opens the same way — learning to introduce yourself, your story, and your reason for being in Japan. The most important conversation you'll have.

3

Customized Syllabus

After Day 1, tutors adapt all learning materials to match your group's specific level and social needs. The curriculum is rebuilt around you — every time.

Weekly Milestones

Your learning path

Two milestone tracks to choose from — or combine both for a full month of immersion.

Week 1–2

Social Survival & Connection

Recommended

Break the ice. Master daily interactions to make local friends at cafes, bars, and community hubs. Practical phrases, real conversations, genuine connections — from day one.

Week 3–4

Academic Mastery — JLPT N5 Path

Structured

Achieve N5 proficiency. Structured grammar and literacy — Hiragana, Katakana, and foundational Kanji — to meet visa requirements and long-term study goals.

Beyond the Classroom

Your classroom is the city

Lessons begin in a relaxed cafe environment. Then we go outside. You don't just learn "how to order" — you go to a local market and do it.

☕

Daily Field Implementation

Every morning lesson is immediately applied in the city — markets, shops, train stations. The living street of Japan is your real classroom.

🏮

Weekend Cultural Immersion

Exclusive weekend activities — traditional craft workshops, hidden local festivals, neighbourhood walks — provide the cultural context for the language you've spent the week learning.

Weekly Schedule

What your week looks like

Mornings in the classroom, afternoons in the city, weekends in the culture.

Mon – Thu

Weekdays

9:00 AM Lesson — 2-hour small-group session in a cafe with 1-hour city walk. Conversation, grammar, real-life scenarios with a local professional teacher.
12:00 PM Field time — afternoon is yours to explore, practice, and live in Japan.
Friday

Rest Day

All day Free day — recharge, explore on your own, or meet up with classmates.
Sat & Sun

Weekend — Language + Culture

9:00 AM 1-hour lesson — focused session to reinforce the week's learning and prepare for the activity ahead.
10:00 AM 2-hour cultural activity — city walks, market visits, temple experiences, craft workshops. Use your Japanese in the real world.
12:00 PM Wrap up — free afternoon to continue exploring at your own pace.
